Our Core Mission

  • To build physical and digital safe spaces to protect the extensive Hubert LaRue historical collection for public use.
  • To acquire historical records, vintage photographs, family bibles, and domestic artifacts detailing life in early Halls Crossroads.
  • To keep admission open to historical investigators, local genealogists, and educational field trips.

About Us & Our Founder

The Halls Crossroads Historical Museum was established to celebrate the spirit, culture, and deep heritage of our region. Operable entirely through public goodwill and volunteer directors, the museum is dedicated to keeping past narratives visible for future generations.

The crown jewel of our institution is the lifelong collection built by our community's foremost preservationist, Hubert LaRue. His collection maps the practical, social, and commercial milestones that shaped our lives.

Hubert LaRue

How We Came to be Halls

The community footprint of Halls Crossroads forms a historical doorway into East Tennessee. Here is an introductory overview of the landmark eras that transformed our settlement:

Late 1700s

The Initial Settlement

Early pioneer families trek across the Appalachian ridges, putting down roots in the fertile basin spaces of East Tennessee near Knox County boundaries.

Early 1800s

The Arrival of Thomas Hall

Thomas Hall arrives in the area and establishes a homestead. His family line forms the backbone of commercial trade and infrastructure at the early intersection.

1900s

The Crossroads Flourishes

As transportation networks map across Tennessee, the area grows into a central commercial hub known widely as "Halls Crossroads," linking outlying farm systems with urban Knoxville markets.

Recent Decades

The Grand Opening

Following decades of archival tracking by regional historians, the community holds its official Grand Opening museum ceremony on Sunday, October 27, drawing over 100 residents to celebrate local history.
